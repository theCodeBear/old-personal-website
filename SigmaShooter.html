Sigma Shooter in Assembly

Sigma Shooter is a console window Shoot 'em Up game I made between late March and mid-April of 2013 as my final project for the Assembly Language course I did as an independent study at Wentorth Institute of Technology. The game is programmed using the MASM Microsoft Assembler in Visual Studio 2010. The source code is just over 2500 lines.

The player controls a ship represented by the Sigma ASCII symbol. The player moves with the arrow keys on the keyboard. The player can shoot lasers and lay bombs. There are enemies that show up in waves and don't have any weapons but do track the player and attempt to dodge lasers and bombs. There are upgrade items that show up occasionally, one upgrade for bombs and one for lasers. Asteroids also fly through the play area, destroying the enemies or the player if they are hit by the asteroid. Asteroids move with various speeds and trajectories across the gameplay screen. There are three gameplay modes, several levels, three color themes, and three difficulty levels to choose from. There is a gametime clock displayed on screen and a point score.
